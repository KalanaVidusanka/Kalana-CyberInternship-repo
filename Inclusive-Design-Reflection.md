# Working with Vulnerable Populations

### Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?

In the digital context, there are can be different groups that are considered as vulnerable populations due various conditions, I believe the below stated populations tend fall and face more challenges in the digital context:

**Children** - Children under the age 10 specifially tend to be developing their thinking pattern, habits, behaviour gradually. Due to this, explicit content or addition to digital space could make their brains shift focus.

**Indiviuals with cognitive/intellectual disabilities** - These indiviuals tend to work and perform their day-to-day actvities in a different manner unique and comfortable for them due to this, indiviuals who are facing these disabilities tend to have learning barriers which can make them get distracted for platforms or need extra focus for tasks. 

**Indiviuals with mental disorders** - These indiviuals could be experincing disorder types such as depression, anxiety that needs constant monitoring and follow-ups in their day-to-day life to support their well-being. Due to this, the content they see in digital spaces could make them more uncomfortable such as hamrful behvaiours through content. Moreover, practicing new habits and shifting focus is a must for indiviuals in this criteria. 

**Indiviuals with excessive additions**- These indiviuals tend to be addicted to certain unethical behaviours that ruin their well-being. As a result, the only path to get them more focusing is new habits in place to change routines and forget the urge they have for their addictions.

**Senior Citizens** - In most cases, senior citizens tend to have low technological background so they tend to adapt and understand the digital space harder. Due to this, they are easy targets for scams, they might share the details to social media mistakenly etc. At some point, they can also get treated with hate speech  and cyberbullying. 

**Communities with barriers to access to devices** - In some cases, indiviuals who are experiencing poverty levels or limited education could also have hardships accessing digital space. Due to this, they won't have the knowledge and the devices to use internet and applications. This can also widen with different communities like LGBTQ+ and different religions etc.

I stated the above groups as they tend to be closely dealing with digital space and experiencing hardships and I believe most of these types of populations can have a connection Focus Bear app to improve their well-being. 

### What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals? 

In this digital era, it is very important to respect every indiviual for their different capabilities, disabilities and their backgrounds. So it is a must to design and work accordingly to support every indiviuals out there. In relation to neurodivergent, it is needed to make them feel comfortable around the application, support them with every possible feature. 

Some of the suggestions when designing and working with these indiviuals could be like:
- **Feature Descriptions** - Every feature can be explained in simple terms to make them understand what is the purpose and how it can supportive for their life which could be illustrated by examples, templates. 
- **Simple UI** - When the UI is easy read and understand, it would be very helpful for all indiviuals. However, too much simiplicity could also be disadvantage as then the needed things in the UI could be missing where as users they have to explore throughout the app to find what they need. 
- **Consent** - It is always great to ask consent before executing or suggesting feature to see if it matches with their goals, background. It always make them comfortable that manner.
- **Disable Option** - It is also great to have an option to disable all possible animations, flashing themes, videos etc. As some indiviuals would find it disturbing to cope with. 
- **Customizable UI** - Offer settings to customize the UI content according to indviual's preference such as Fonts, Theme colors, Light or Dark Mode, audio controls etc.
- **Language** - Use respectful and simple language throughout the UI. Also, make sure to avoid words that explain the disabilities as a negative thing or a disorder instead always showing positive aspect og how they can improve better. 
- **Voice commands** - To support indiviuals who tend to prefer audio interpretations, an option to support text-to-speech for UI content could be helpful and this option can be supportive for any indiviual in different cases for their purpose. 
- **Feedback** - It is a great way to improve by gaining feedback from neurodivergent indiviuals on how productivity or easy they tend every feature. This will help to improve current and future tasks. 
- **Neurodivergent Perspective** - Always try to think from their perspective and offer the services tailored to them that also aligns with non-neurodivergent. Never use servcies or langauge that makes them feel as if they are dealing with something that is negative for this society or them. 

 ### How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism?

 - **Simple UI** - When the UI is simple to look at and use, people with ADHD or Autism would find it easy to cope with and find their needed services from the UI. 
 - **Simple Language** - Using direct and simple language would make them understand what the feature does without having to think deeply. Also. use short phases or paragraphs to make it simple to read and understand.
 - **Feature Priority** - Tailor the features according to the priority of the indiviual's preference and inputs so they can easily get going with what is needed without dealing with every feature to find what they need.
 - **Guided Steps** - Use proper steps with numbered points stating what needs to be done exactly to avoid confusion or overwhelm them. 
- **Data Viualization** - Simple charts can be used to showcase the evidence of a feature or a service to maximise the understanding. 
- **Disable Option** - Intergrate the disabling option for different concepts or animations in the UI.
- Transcripts - Intergrate transcripts for video and audio visualizations so that users can view them later as well. 
- Plain Colors - Use simple and plain backgrounds to avoid too much messy or high contrast UI. Also use warm matching color palette across different options, pages etc.
- Task breakdown - Intergrate an option to breakdown major tasks to simple easy tasks to execute to boost productivity. 
- Avoid too many pages or clicks to complete a task or set a habit. As it can overwhelm the user. Simple clicks that lead to final outcome would boost the usuage as well. 
- Automatic Saves - If an user has started a task or a feature and he/she cannot complete it in the first go, having automatic save can them complete it at a later time and they don't have to worry about redoing some things from the beginning. 
- Consistent Format - Enable the same format throughout the application UI so users won't feel lost across the UI.

### What are some common UX or communication pitfalls that might make Focus Bear less accessible or supportive?

- Promoting advertisements for the users across the application. 
- Lack of options or settings to customize the UI or the features to fit indiviual preferences. 
- Too much detailed paragraphs for each feature that takes time to read and understand. 
- Negative language or features that frame different disabilities of users to make them feel uncomfortable. 
- High Contrast colors and themes that make users feel stressed and uncomfortable to operate the UI.
- Too many technical terms or words that needs further findings which could be a hassle to find and understand as well. 
- Providing too many videos or audios for every feature or task could also overwhelm the user. 
- Presenting all the features and services at the first sign up could also confuse and overwhelm the user. 
- Lack of UI consistency across pages and options with different buttons, themes etc. 
- No proper notifications about app updates or patches which might interupt the user's daily usage incase of an update without their attention. 
- Predicting that the UI setup would fit for all users out there which is not valid as different users have different preferences. 
- Lack of offline capabilities as some users tend to prefer and except to use applications offline.
- Easy Troubleshoot guidelines or fixes for any accident errors or configurations with featues. 
- Missing many accssibility features for different indiviuals which can disrupt them making it harder for them to find what is needed. 
- There are features or services that are out of the context of focusing, productivity etc.
