### What types of information should never be entered into AI tools? :warning:

The information types that should never be entered or communicated with AI or agents are:

- **PII (Personally Identifiable Information)** - These are the critical information of humans. As this category includes are full names, housing addresses, All Government based licenses with their ID numbers, personal contact numbers, Email Addresses. The deeper and the more crucial this category can even point out sensitive data such as Fingerprints, Facial recognition scans. 
- **Financial Information** -These are the information relating to bank accounts such as account numbers, CVVs, expiry dates and billing addresses. Also, never upload any bank statements or pay slips to AI tools.
- **Organization Data** - These are the type of crucial documents owned by an organization such as network infrastructure plans, software manuals, buisness roadmaps, meeting notes, HR documents, employee records etc.
- **Personal Accounts** - These are the type of data that deals with social media accounts or any website logins such as usernames, passwords, pins etc. 
- **Medical Records** - These are the type of data that deals with patient records, scans, insurance information etc. 
- **Legal Documents** - These are the type of data that is used in the context of law such as housing deeds, contracts, agreements, digital signatures etc. 

Best practice is to never share any type of data that is linked with your personal accounts, life or the workplace to safeguard the data from getting stored in an unknown database or getting leaked to the public at sometime. 

### How can you fact-check and validate AI-generated content to ensure accuracy? :white_check_mark:

Personally, I prefer to always proof read all the content generated by an AI tool to see if there are any errors or misconfigurations. As an example, if a PDF is getting analyzed by an AI tool, I would make sure to skim through the document to find key points so that I can tally with the AI output to see if they match and there are no made up concepts. 

In regards to information accuracy, google search can be conducted directly with the output to match whether the data is correct. In relation to figures, it is good to check other research articles on google scholar or other websites that the same figure is seen across or only with a minor difference. However, if there happens to be a big difference that means AI content had a misread and performed negatively. 

Moreover, the same query or the prompt can be distributed among 2-3 AI tools to compare the outputs as well to see how well they have explained, what are the areas missing in any tool and see if the content and the justification match across all tools.

Another important approach is always prompting to output with references and citations and if possible with redirecting links so you can actually visit the source to confirm that the content is accurate and the reference is taken from it as well.
