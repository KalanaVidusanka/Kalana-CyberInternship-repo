### How should you respond to a suspected data breach or accidental disclosure of confidential information?

In relation to a suspected data breach or accidental leakage of confidential information it is always good to not panic at once and unecessary actions that might disrupt further. 

In a scenario like a data breach:
1. As a member, the major changes or suspicious behaviour should be closely monitored and taken notes down to not miss anything. 
2. Inform the respected parties such as supervisors and the CEOs. 
3. Take possible steps to minimize the data breach such as turning off possible servers or devices if it is safe to do or as per the instructions. 
4. Try to connect the dots on how the data breach occured and document a clear timeline till the present such as affected devices, what data is affected etc.
5. Notify all team members regarding the situation to warn them to avoid further loss or attacks. 
6. Work closely with the supervisors and security teams to fully explain them regarding how and what has happened. 

I believe it is good to maintain good security habits to avoid data breaches and leakage and also having a securtiy awareness and how to adapt incase of an attack will make sure the organization, data and members have a good upper hand incase of an attack.

### How should you store, share, and dispose of sensitive information safely?

**Storage of Sensitive information**:
- Store only Focus Bear approved platforms and strictly limit permissions to general viewers outside Focus Bear. 
- Check if the storage platform offers good security encyrption. 
- Never store sensitive information on pen drives or CDs that can get misplaced or stolen.
- Never store on emails, SMS chats etc. 
- Inform the relevant parties that these type of data is stored on your end for this specifc task or project. 
- Never store them on AI tools. 
- Never upload the data to websites or platforms for personal gain. 
- Implement strong access controls. 

**Sharing of Sensitive Information**:
- Never share sensitive inforrmation through emails, SMS. 
- Use encrypted platforms that use transit encryption methods. 
- Share data only with authorized indiviuals. 
- Never share data with friends, family or general public. 
- Share only the needed amount of data.
- Review whether you have the authority to share, even if you have it is good to inform that the data is shared. 
- Try to lock the data or files with passwords so that only the receiver can view or modify it. 
  
**Disposing of Sensitive Information**:
- Align with the legal and cyber framework to dispose data accurately. 
- Dispose data exactly to the given timelines and instructions.
- Use secure deletion softwares. 
- Clear version history of disposing data. 
- Inform the team or users that data will be deleted and no longer stored. 
- Document all the processes, date and data when it was disposed. 
- Delete the disposing data from backups as well. 
- Use the Focus Bear guidelines and approved methods to dispose data. 
- Never store or gain data by faking the dispose. 

### What are some common mistakes that lead to data privacy issues, and how can they be avoided?

| **Common Mistakes for Data Privacy Issues** | **How to Avoid Them** |
| :-------------------------------------: | :----------------:|
| Weak Passwords or same password across all accounts | Use unique passwords for every account with mininum 12 characters with uppercase and lowercase letters, symbols and numbers. Use Password Managers |
| Collecting or sharing too much data out of the scope | Specify what type of data is collected, stored and processed. |
| Sharing sensitive data through emails, SMS, physical documents or external devices | Use secured mediums and never share data outside the company |
| Having poor security measures in devices, internet, softwares | Implement screen locks, max password attempt, update broadband passwords and protocls, update latest system and software updates across all devices and softwares |
| Linking unwanted links, attachments or websites | Reviewing the email carefully and checking if there were past conversation with the specific email to verify, Delete the email if it feels suspcious or scam type, Visit and work only on websites with "Paddlock Icon" and SSL digital certificate, Never click on pop-up ads in websites, apps or games |
| Storing sensitive data in general platforms and places | Use encrypted mediums to store data, limit permission for the stored data, review if major changes has occured for the stored data |
| Avoding updates regarding major changes or modifications of data | Update the relevant parties and teams and be honest if something wrong happened |
| Not aligning or implementing standard cybersecurity measures | Implement and train all members with cyber frameworks and legal aspects, conduct security awareness programs |
