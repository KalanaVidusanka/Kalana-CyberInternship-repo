### What does respectful communication look like in remote teams? 

As remote teams tend to work via virtual workplaces such as for communication different apps like Google Meet, Zoom and other shareable platforms that are interconnected with everyone on the team to work collaboratively. 

Some ways to be respectful in remote environments are:
- Using positive tone when speaking with other team members. 
- Actively listening when someone is speaking without interrupting. 
- Always come to conclusion that aligns and agrees across all team members. 
- Receive the consent or feedback before executing a major update or change. 
- Always do the necessary changes given by the feedback and also give feedback in a positive welcoming manner. 
- Don't always expect instant replies and give the team sometime to process and also they are dealing with their own day-to-day tasks. 
- Always use the right platform for the right type of deliverables. Never share sensitive conversations or private talks with other team members or data. 
- Acknowledge all messages even by a small word as "Noted" to make the team be aware that you read and agreed.
- Try to always build up a genuine connection and impression with other team members without directly seeking work from them. 
- Plan everything in advance and make sure everyone is on the same page to make everyone comfortable. 

### How can you give and receive feedback professionally? 

**Sending Feedback**:
- Below sending a feedback, read and see whether as a receiver would I like to read this, is this positive or does it give a bad intention or a frustation. After reviewing, comment on the good points/content the member have done and show how it can be improved further if needed. If there are errors or content should be removed suggest alternate ways to do it. Suggest sources to look for to improve it. Instead of directly saying wrong or disagreeing try to show the member how it can be approached in another way and how it will impact better. In addition, check which type of feedback style is necessary is it public or private. 

**Receiving Feedback**: 
- It is a must appreciate the effort by other person for giving the feedback. Reply back highlighting the key points to improve by stating how you will improve and when you will improve along with an update again. Don't jump to arguments or conclusion without understanding the whole feedback. Seek further guidance if needed for clarifications. It is also ok to suggest some time to think the feedback and that response will be given soon. Do a positive apology if there are major errors.

### What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?

Communication can happen in different ways in different times of the day. So below are some general steps that would apply any medium:

1. Understanding the audience whether they know this topic or should I first educate them and the purpose of what needs to done whether the receiver needs to do anything, understand something or support a decision.
2. Be prepared with what will be delivered, what are the points that will be presented.
3. Use simple langugae and less technical terms as audience might have indiviuals who are not in the same industry. 
4. Be specific about points, work or decisions to keep it direct. 
5. Clearly explain every point so the audience understands. 
6. Summarize the key takeaways or actions at the end. 
7. If someone else is speaking, first listen and then speak after they are done.
8. Don't disgrace with the character but raise counter points for the idea or opinion positively. 
9. Try to match the tone of the other party to make them feel comfortable.
10. Gain feedback whether they understood what you said and if there were confusions, clarify positively.

### How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?

- Editing the Meeting invite with the project name and a short brief or agenda. 
- Preparing an agenda on what will be discussed and presented to speak all the important points and not to miss any. 
- Presenting the solutions or blockers directly avoiding any unnecessary discussions.
- Taking notes on all meetings. If it is hard to focus while taking notes, it is good to use an AI notetaker. 
- Listening to the feedback or other members when presenting without working on different tasks. 
- Don't interrupt when someone is presenting their solutions, ideas etc. 
- Keeping and asking questions that needs direct human decisions or opinions or if it is something seek help from AI or google resources. 
- Speaking or messaging in the meeting chat about any update or challenge without being silent. 
- Reviewing any documents or reports needed for the specific meeting to understand and present clearly. 
- Research any difficult areas to get a base understanding and seek it. 
- Arriving on time for the meetings. 
- Summarize the key points presented and also sneding a follow-up after the meeting. 
- Being honest about progress or solutions. 
- Seek direct feedback on anything to be improved.
