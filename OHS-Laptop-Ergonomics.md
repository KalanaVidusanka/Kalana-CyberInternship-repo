# Occupational Health & Safety (OHS) for Desk-Based Work

### What are the risks of using a laptop without an external monitor or keyboard?

When working only with a laptop throughout the day it will be tiring both physically and mentally. As laptop tends to be a device that is portable for the best use on the go or to do general tasks but not recommended for longer hours. 

**Physical Risks**:
- **Neck and Head Pain** - As the laptop screen is small or sitting on a lower position, you tend to bend your neck and body to look closely and you will be continuing a bad posture. Due to this, it can affect your neck, give you headaches etc. 
- **Digital Eye Strain** - Due to long hours of close contact with the laptop screen, you can develop eye strain easily as well making tired and dry eyes the next day. 
- **Hand Pain** - Sometimes users tend to use the laptop for longer times with their built-in keyboards and trackpads that can cause pain as well due to bad hand positions and angles. 
- **Shoulder and Spine Pain** - As the body tends to be bended, it can cause shoulder pain and spine pain that might need treatments or meds in the long run. 

**Mental and other Risks**:
- **Limited Screen Space** - As laptops tend to be smaller in size compared to monitors there screen is limited to one application at a time. Due to this working, organizing and viewing your work can create frustation and stress. 
- **Fatigue** - Laptops could cause fatigue and loss of motivation as the content seems to be small and working long hours makes you tiring. 
- Hard to multitask with 2 projects or reports at a time as new screens or desktops should be opened which can also make the laptop perform less sometimes. 
- In the long run, it could lead or affect to other diseases or injuiries that could be serious. 

### What equipment changes can you make to improve your workspace setup?

**Computer/Gaming Chairs** - These types of chairs are built with the design in mind to support consumers to remain in their seat for a longer period of time without any pain or long term injuries. Some chairs include built in lumber support, foot rest, angles etc. 

**External Monitors** - Connecting a monitor to the laptop or having dual monitors would make the work easier and improve posture as wider screens and more space to work freely. 

**Laptop stands** - Laptop stands can be used to lift the laptop to a moderate position aligned with your posture. 

**Standing Desks** - These desks can be used while standing to avoid bad posture. However, long hours of standing can also be a pain but these desks tend to have options to lower the table to fit a chair so you can use it both ways. 

**Keyboards and mouses** - Using these it can help with building a good posture. Most of the gaming items tend to have in-built support to rest your wrist when typing or using the mouse with a comfortable mouse pad. 

**Blue Light Glasses** - Using blue light glasses can support to due the eye strain when using the devices. 

**Foot Rests Or Mats** - There are comfortable foot rests and mats that can be adjusted with height to support legs and to improve overall posture. 


### What adjustments should be made to monitor height, chair position, and desk setup for a healthier workspace?

Implementing small adjustments to accessories and devices can impact your health very positively.

Below are some ways to adjust them:

- **Monitor Height** - Adjust the height for your comfortable eye level where you can see the center of the monitor without any hassle or pain in a restful position. Make sure the monitor is not tilted to one side more. Try using an external monitpr arm or holder.
- **Chair position** - Lift the chair to a level where you can use the keyboard and mouse freely and the monitor is visible at a good level without having to look up or down. Make sure the legs can be kept on the floor or a foot rest. Sit straight on the chair without bending too much. Use cushion for lumber support and neck support if needed.
- **Desk Setup** - Make sure the desk is supporting your hands to work easily with the external devices. 90 degree angle on your hands when working on the desk is ideal. Keep the mouse and keyboard at a very reachable distance.
